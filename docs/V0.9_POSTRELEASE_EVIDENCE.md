# Super SOL v0.9 post-release evidence

Date: 2026-07-16

## Decision

Keep `v0.9.0-rc1` frozen as a prerelease. Do not create `0.9.1` from T149-T160, do not promote the
candidate to stable, and do not claim quality uplift. `v0.8.0` remains the stable release.

The bounded positive claim is:

> On the preregistered T149-T160 crossover, Super SOL shared the leading quality tier with raw
> Codex and SOLTELU and was the token/time efficiency leader within that tier. A unique quality
> winner and a strictly positive quality uplift were not established.

## Design and audit

- Profiles: GPT-5.6 Sol/high and GPT-5.6 Terra/xhigh
- Products: raw Codex, Super SOL, SOLTELU, and LazyCodex
- Tasks: T149-T160, authored and sealed after the product tag
- Repetitions: 2
- Lattice: 8 cells x 12 tasks x 2 repetitions = 192 valid slots
- Attempts: 192 valid, 0 censored, 0 retries, 0 running
- Isolation: fresh Orca worktree and sanitized physical `CODEX_HOME` per slot
- Audit: passed every lattice, provenance, prompt, runtime, grader, and attempt-history check

The original 32-slot Gate 2 in `V0.9_PROMOTION_PROTOCOL.md` remains `NOT RUN`. This extended
crossover is reported as a separate study rather than retroactively changing the old gate.

## Claim boundaries

- Allowed: shared quality tier on this task pack; mean token/time efficiency leader within that tier.
- Allowed: positive point estimates whose confidence intervals include zero.
- Not allowed: universal quality winner, capability-ceiling increase, or proven quality uplift.
- Not allowed: pooling this score with the v0.8 Fablize result or comparing provider-native tokens.
- Not allowed: tuning `0.9.1` on T149-T160 and reusing those tasks as confirmation.
